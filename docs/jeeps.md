### Critical Recalls Detail

**18V278000 + 12V085000 — Rear Lower Control Arm**
- Arms fracture from road salt corrosion with zero warning at any speed
- Expansion recall (U38) covers vehicles not caught in original L27 campaign
- Geographic restriction on 12V085: CT, DE, IL, IN, IA, ME, MD, MA, MI, MN, MO, NH, NJ, NY, OH, PA, RI, VT, WV, WI, DC — **Indiana IS covered**
- Remedy: free replacement at any Jeep/Chrysler dealer

**13V252000 — Fuel Tank Fire**
- 1.56 million vehicles affected (2002–2007 Liberty + Grand Cherokee)
- Risk: rear collision ruptures tank near ignition source
- Remedy: dealer installs Chrysler tow hitch as rear-impact guard, free of charge
- Recall began August 1, 2014 — verify completion

## COMMON PROBLEMS — 2007 Jeep Liberty KJ

### Safety-Critical
- **Rear lower control arms** — corrosion fracture, sudden loss of control (recall-related)
- **Lower ball joints** — moisture intrusion, premature wear, wheel separation risk
- **Underbody/frame rust** — especially severe in northern/salt-belt states (both Jeeps from Toledo, OH)

### Mechanical
- **Window regulators** — extremely common failure, windows drop into door panels
- **Rear differential** — weak point on 4WD models
- **Stalling/hesitation** — throttle body, TPS, or transmission-related
- **Fuel burping** — gas splashes back during refueling (widespread complaint)

### Electrical
- **TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module)** — common Chrysler-era electrical gremlin
- Instrument cluster issues, sensor faults, lighting problems
- Clock spring failures (airbag/horn circuit)

### Cosmetic
- **Paint peeling** — hood and roof most commonly reported
- **Frame/undercarriage rust** — inspect thoroughly before any work
